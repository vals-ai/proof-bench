Let $Y=\text{Spec}(A)$ and $Z = \text{Spec}(\mathbb{Z})$ (the terminal object), let $f: X \to Y$ be the structure morphism of $X$ as an $A$-scheme, let $g: Y \to Z$ be the morphism $\text{Spec}(A) \to \text{Spec}(\mathbb{Z})$

The key point is the following cancellation property for separatedness: If $g$ is separated, then $(g \circ f)$ is separated $\iff$ $f$ is separated.

The composition $(g \circ f): X \to Z$ is the structure morphism of $X$ as a $\mathbb{Z}$-scheme. Since the $g$ is a morphism between affine schemes, it is automatically separated, the proposition applies.

Therefore, we conclude that $X$ is separated over $\mathbb{Z}$ $\iff$ $X$ is separated over $A$.
